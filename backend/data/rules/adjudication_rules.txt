ADJUDICATION RULES — Blue Shield Insurance
Version: 2024.1
Effective: January 1, 2024

AUTO-APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
- Claims with total billed amount of $25,000 or less are eligible for automatic approval if all clinical and administrative checks pass.
- Claims between $25,001 and $75,000 must be flagged for human review regardless of check results.
- Claims above $75,000 must be escalated to senior adjudicator and are never auto-approved.

MANDATORY DENIAL CONDITIONS
Any of the following require automatic denial:
1. Clinical mismatch: The primary diagnosis does not clinically support the billed procedure code. This is a non-waivable denial.
2. Missing prior authorization for a procedure that requires it, where no auth number is present in the claim.
3. Procedure billed is explicitly excluded under the patient's benefit plan.
4. Duplicate claim: Same patient, same procedure, same date of service as a previously processed claim.
5. Provider is not credentialed or has been excluded from the network.

HUMAN REVIEW TRIGGERS
Route to human review when:
- Claim amount exceeds $25,000 even if all checks pass.
- Two or more WARN flags are raised by the intake agent.
- Diagnosis code is a WARN-level match rather than a clear PASS or FAIL.
- Provider has had more than 2 claims in the past 30 days for the same procedure.

DISPUTE AND APPEAL
- Denied claims may be appealed within 60 days of denial notice.
- Human review decisions are final unless escalated by supervisor.

GENERAL PRINCIPLES
- All decisions must be based on the benefit plan in effect on the date of service.
- Benefit plan PPO-2024 is the active plan for all claims dated January 1, 2024 onward.
- When in doubt, route to human review rather than auto-approve or auto-deny.
